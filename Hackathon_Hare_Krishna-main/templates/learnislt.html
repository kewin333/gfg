EXAMPLES

Here are some of the examples of sign language with the description/ explanation of the individual signs.

Cool

The sign for "cool" usually involves placing the tips of the index and middle fingers of one hand on the side of the forehead, while the thumb is extended upwards.<
The hand is then moved slightly away from the forehead, while the fingers and thumb remain in the same position.
This sign is often used to convey a sense of admiration or approval, particularly in the context of fashion or style. It can also be used more generally to convey a sense of something being trendy or fashionable

Breakfast

To sign "breakfast" in Indian Sign Language (ISL), you can use both hands.
Start with both hands in a fist, with your thumbs pointing up.
Then, bring your fists up to your forehead and tap your forehead with your thumbs twice.
This sign represents the idea of "breaking the fast" after sleeping, as breakfast is typically the first meal of the day after a period of not eating during sleep.
It's important to note that Indian Sign Language is a rich and diverse language with regional variations, so the specific sign for "breakfast" may vary depending on the region or community.

Camera

To sign "camera" in Indian Sign Language (ISL), you can follow these steps:
Hold one hand in front of you, with the fingers together and the thumb pointing up.
Use the other hand to mimic pressing the button on the top of the camera.
Then, bring the hand with the button down towards the other hand, as if you are taking a picture.
The sign for "camera" in ISL represents the physical camera as a device used for taking pictures or recording videos.

Arrogant

Start with the dominant hand open, fingers together, and palm facing downward.
Place the dominant hand on the forehead, with the fingertips pointing upwards and the palm facing away from the body.
Then move the hand forward and down, in a sweeping motion, while closing the fingers into a fist.
This sign is used to convey the meaning of someone being arrogant or conceited, and it suggests the gesture of someone brushing their hair back while displaying a superior attitude.
Please note that Indian Sign Language may vary slightly depending on the region and community, and it is best to consult with a qualified Indian Sign Language interpreter for accurate representation and proper usage of sign language.
